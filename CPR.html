  1. Survey the scene to make an initial assessment. Put on gloves!
  2. Kneel down next to the person to check for responsiveness. For adults, this means tapping their shoulder and shouting their name. For small children, tap the bottom of their feet instead of their shoulder.
  3. If the person is unresponsive, immediately call 911. Pointing at and assigning this role to a specific person reduces the risk of the bystander effect. If nobody was directly asked to call 911, it may lead to a delay in action.
  4. Check for pulse and breathing. When checking for pulse, place two fingers (not the thumb) on the side of the neck. Tilt the head back with a palm on the forehead and with fingers lifting the chin. Place your ear next to their mouth to listen for breathing while watching if chest inflates.
